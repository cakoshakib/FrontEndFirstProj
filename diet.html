Diet

Bamboo

Pandas are known for eating bamboo all day; more specifically 16 to 24 hours a day. It is not surprising as 99% of their diet is bamboo. Bamboo became their official diet as far back as three million years ago. However, their carnivorous digestive system makes it difficult to digest the needed nutrients needed as bamboo is mostly composed of cellulose. As a result, they eat a great amount of grass to make up for this loss. During spring, the pandas would eat young wood bamboo shoots which have phosphorus and nitrogen. In June, the pandas migrate to higher elevations to eat young arrow bamboo shoots to obtain a sufficient amount of calcium.

Meat and Calcium

Pandas are bears, like other bears they have a digestive system that is meant to break down meat. However, since 99% of their diet is bamboo they lack a lot of nutrients, especially calcium. Pandas would not be able to survive outside of their habitat. Sometimes, pandas resort to licking rocks to gain the calcium needed to survive. Their diet has played a main part in why they are endangered, they simply lack the nutrients needed to survive.
